Key in hand

Keys are familiar and very valuable things that protect important people and places in our lives. They also inspire us to open doors to unknown worlds. With these considerations in mind, in this new installation I would like to use the keys of the general public which are imbued with various memories and memories that have accumulated over a long period of daily use. As I create work in space, the memories of everyone who gives me their keys for the first time will overlap with my own memories. These overlapping memories will in turn combine with the memories of people from all over the world to come to see the biennale, giving them the opportunity to communicate in a new way and better understand each other's feelings. In this exhibition, Shiota will integrate the gallery located on the second floor and the open pilot on the first floor of the Japanese Pavilion. Upon entering the gallery, viewers will find a space filled with red yarn. Attached at the end of each piece of yarn, hanging from the ceiling, will be a key. In our daily life, keys protect valuable things like our homes, possessions and personal safety, and we use them as we hug them in the warmth of our arms.
