Salvador Dali

Salvador Dali, born in 1904 in Spain, was one of the most famous and controversial artists of the 20th century. Dali was a pioneer of Surrealism and is often considered a visionary and a genius. His paintings and sculptures are characterized extraordinary precision, absurd elements, unexpected combinations of objects and extremely rich symbolism. Dali was a master of the "paranoid-critical method" technique, through which he used hallucinations and dreams as a source inspiration for his works. His works often depict transformed landscapes, deformed figures and bizarre iconography. Dalí is also known for his eccentric appearance, including his signature mustache that has become his trademark. His most famous painting, "See the Apparition and Become It," illustrates his fascination with illusion, deep thinking and deep meaning behind visual images. Was he an artist? who provoked, challenged and explored the boundaries of mind and reality. Although he died in 1989, his legacy i influence on contemporary art remain deeply rooted.
