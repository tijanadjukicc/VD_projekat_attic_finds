Self portrait

Like Rembrandt van Rijn and Francisco Goya, Vincent van Gogh often used himself as a model. In an incredible series of candid self-portraits, he charts his development as an artist. His self-portraits not only record changes in his painting technique, but also reveal his a mental breakdown with a humility and sincerity not seen since Rembrandt's self-portrait. In the last five years of his life, he painted over thirty self-portraits. His painting Self-Portrait, also known by the name The portrait of the artist, which may be his last self-portrait, was painted in 1889, shortly before than he went to the sanatorium of Saint Remy in Provence in the south of France. This painting is now in the museum Orsay in Paris.
