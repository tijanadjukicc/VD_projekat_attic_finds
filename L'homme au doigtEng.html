L'homme au doigt

L'Homme au doigt ("The Man with the Finger"; also called the Pointing Man) is a bronze sculpture Albert Giacometti from 1947, which became the most expensive sculpture ever when it sold for $141.3 million May 11, 2015. Giacometti made six casts of the work plus one artist's proof. Pointing Man is in the collections of New York's Museum of Modern Art, London's Tate Gallery and the Des Moines Art Center. One of the others is also in the museum, and the others are in foundation collections or are privately owned. L'homme au doigt sold for $126 million, or $141.3 million with royalties, at Christie's on May 11, 2015 In Search of the Past in New York, a record for sculpture at the auction. The work was in the private collection of Sheldon Solow for 45 years. Christie's called it a "rare masterpiece" and "Giacometti's most iconic and interesting sculpture", i estimated to sell "in the region of $130 million". Christie's also noted that it is believed that the cast in their auction is the only one that Giacometti "painted by hand to enhance its expressive influence".
