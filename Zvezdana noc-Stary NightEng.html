Stary Night

Starry Night is one of Vincent van Gogh's most famous paintings and marks a decisive turn towards the greater freedom of imagination in his art. Photographed in June 1889, it shows the view from a window at night sanatorium rooms at Saint Remy in Provence in southern France, although it was painted from memory during the day. Since 1941 This painting is in the permanent collection of the Museum of Modern Art in New York. In May 1889, Vincent van Gogh decided to go to a sanatorium in Saint Remy, where he stayed until next year. Inspired by the landscape surrounding the sanatorium, he painted Starry Night a month later arrival and 13 months before suicide. In that period, Vincent writes to his brother Theo: "It is an eternal question, whether we are given yes before death we know only one hemisphere. As far as I'm concerned, there are no answers, but the stars always make me think." The painting Starry Night shows a rural landscape at night, with hills in the background. A scene of agitated flickering of the elements of nature and the human world is created, while the village stretches towards to the sky with spiral and tangled movements.
