Van Gogh

Vincent van Gogh, born in 1853 in the Netherlands, was one of the most influential artists of the modern era. Van Gogh was known for his bright, expressive paintings and a style that stood out from his traditional painting times. His works, among which the most famous cycles of flower paintings and self-portraits, are characteristic by the vivid color palette and unusual use of light and shadow. Van Gogh suffered from depression throughout his life and mental disorders, and at one point he was even placed in a mental institution. Nevertheless, despite all the difficulties, he succeeded is to create a significant artistic legacy that still inspires many today. His most famous painting, "Starry Night", it is made of densely strung brushes that create a turbulent and exciting effect. Van Gogh died in 1890, at only 37 years of age, but he left behind a lasting legacy that still inspires many today artist.
