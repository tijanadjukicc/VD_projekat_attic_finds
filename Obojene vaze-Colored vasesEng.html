Colored vases

In 1994, famous Chinese artist Ai Weiwei decorated a Han dynasty urn with the red Coca-Cola logo; the following year he was photographed dropping and smashing another. Colored Vases (2009-10), featured in an artist show at Leeson Gallery, London (until July 16), continue in the same spirit of protest. Despite their seemingly innocent pigmentation, iridescent industrial paint damage to these precious the Han dynasty vessel forms part of the artist's larger critique of the cultural and historical vandalism of the Chinese state. On April 2, the State Councilor for Culture of the People's Republic of China opened the first international exhibition in the newly renovated To the National Museum of China entitled Art of Enlightenment. The very next day, in a disturbing synchronicity, Chinese police arrested Ai in Beijing as he was about to board a flight to Hong Kong. No official information about his whereabouts or the reasons for his detention have been given. and at the time of going to press he had been in prison for 47 days without charge.
