Claude Monet

Claude Monet, born in 1840 in Paris, was a French impressionist painter whose art changed the landscape of painting forever. Monet was a pioneer in his use of color and light, and is known for painting everyday scenes of nature and urban landscapes. Throughout his career, Monet developed a distinctive style that reflected his passion for capturing the moment and visual impression. His series of paintings depicting water locations, such as the Seine, Giverny and his most famous motif, water lilies, left an indelible impression on 19th and 20th century art. Monet was a leader of the Impressionist movement and his art influenced generations of painters after him. His tireless desire to explore light and color makes him one of the most respected artists of all time, whose paintings are now in museums around the world. Claude Monet died in 1926, but his legacy and influence remain indelible.
